## Cohort-Based vs Open Access Analysis

### Why Do Courses Use Cohorts?

**1. Community & Accountability**
- Cohorts create peer pressure and social motivation
- Students have classmates at the same stage
- Discussion forums are active because everyone's on the same material
- Built-in study groups and networking opportunities

**2. Live Interaction**
- Office hours, Q&A sessions, live workshops
- Real-time feedback from instructors
- Ability to adjust teaching based on common struggles
- Creates "event" energy around the course

**3. Scarcity & Urgency**
- "Enrolment closes Friday" drives action
- Limited seats justify premium pricing
- Creates FOMO for future cohorts
- Each cohort feels exclusive

**4. Structured Pacing**
- Students can't skip ahead or fall behind
- Everyone's discussing the same content
- Prevents the "I'll do it later" procrastination
- Creates natural deadlines

**5. Higher Completion Rates**
- Accountability to peers increases follow-through
- Scheduled sessions prevent abandonment
- Social proof of others progressing motivates continuation
- Typically 10-30% completion vs 3-5% for self-paced

**6. Premium Pricing Justification**
- Live elements justify Â£500-5000+ pricing
- "Access to instructor" is valuable positioning
- Community membership has ongoing value
- Feels more like education, less like content consumption

### Why Open Access Could Be Better for dÃ—e

**1. Our Content Structure**
- 150+ lessons across multiple tracks is a lot to cohort
- Students have different starting points (designer vs engineer)
- Platform tracks (Web/iOS/Android) are independent paths
- Not everyone needs the same material

**2. Reference vs Course**
- This is designed as a career-long resource
- People will return to specific lessons during projects
- Cohort pacing doesn't fit "I need to learn X right now for work"
- Value comes from depth and completeness, not community

**3. Global Audience**
- Time zones make live sessions exclusionary
- Async-first is more accessible
- No "you missed the cohort" friction
- Always available when someone's ready to learn

**4. Our Business Model**
- One-time purchase is simpler than recurring cohorts
- No operational burden of running cohort cycles
- Content updates benefit all customers, not just current cohort
- Scales infinitely without additional effort

**5. The Hybrid Role Itself**
- Design Engineers are self-directed learners
- They figure things out independently â€” that's the job
- Heavy hand-holding may attract wrong audience
- Self-paced proves self-motivation

### Potential Hybrid Approach

If we wanted some cohort benefits without full cohort model:

**Option A: Cohort-Optional Community**
- Course is open access
- Optional paid Discord/Slack community
- Quarterly "learning sprints" for those who want accountability
- Live Q&As available to all, recorded for async

**Option B: Cohort Launches, Open Evergreen**
- Launch in cohorts (creates urgency and community)
- After cohort completes, course becomes evergreen
- Previous cohort members form ongoing community
- New evergreen purchasers can join community for extra fee

**Option C: Certification Cohorts**
- Course is open access for learning
- Optional cohort-based certification track
- Certification includes project review, live feedback
- Premium pricing for certification, not content

### Recommendation

**Start with open access.** Here's why:

1. **Lower operational burden** â€” Running cohorts is a job
2. **Matches content structure** â€” Multi-track, multi-platform doesn't fit cohort pacing
3. **Tests market first** â€” Prove demand before adding complexity
4. **Fits the audience** â€” DEs are self-starters by definition

**Add community later if there's demand:**
- Launch with optional Discord
- Monitor engagement and requests
- If people want accountability, offer it as add-on
- Don't force structure on people who don't need it

**Reserve cohorts for advanced offerings:**
- Future masterclasses or workshops
- Project-based intensive programmes
- 1:1 or small-group mentorship
- Career transition programmes

The core course should be accessible to anyone, anytime. That's the whole point of democratising this knowledge.
